Layer chickens are a special species of hens, which want to be raised from when they are one day old and they start laying eggs commercially from 18 to 19 weeks of age. They remain to lay eggs continuously till their 72 to 78 weeks of age. They can generate about one kg of eggs by consuming about 2.25 kg of food during their egg-laying period. To produce a hybrid egg layer, consider the different characteristics of cock and hen before breeding. There are different types of highly egg productive layer breeds available throughout the world.
Eggs are laid by female animals of many different species, including birds, reptiles, amphibians, a few mammals, and fish, and many of these have been eaten by humans for thousands of years. Bird and reptile eggs consist of a protective eggshell, albumen (egg white), and vitellus (egg yolk), contained within various thin membranes. The most commonly consumed eggs are chicken eggs. Other poultry eggs including those of duck and quail also are eaten.
